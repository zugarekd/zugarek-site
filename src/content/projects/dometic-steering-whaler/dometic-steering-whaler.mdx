### ESP32 Feeding Simulated NMEA 2000 Data

To bench test my helm electronics without launching the boat, I built a small ESP32-based NMEA 2000 message generator. It simulates engine RPM, speed over water (STW/SOW), heading, and other data directly onto the CAN bus.

## üîß ESP32 NMEA 2000 Sender (RPM Simulation)

The ESP32 sends PGN 127488 (Engine Rapid Update) to simulate engine RPM and PGN 128259 for speed over water. This allows the helm display and autopilot system to behave as if the engine is idling on the water.

![ESP32 NMEA2000 Sender](/projects/dometic-steering-whaler/ESP32_sender.jpg)

---

## üõú ESP32 CAN Bus Interface

The ESP32 connects to the NMEA 2000 backbone via a CAN transceiver. The board acts as a full NMEA 2000 node, broadcasting structured PGNs on the bus.

This allows full bench testing of:

- RPM display
- Speed over water
- Heading
- Autopilot idle detection logic
- Steering stop calibration

![ESP32 CAN Bus Wiring](/projects/dometic-steering-whaler/CAN.jpg)
